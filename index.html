About Us

Mission Statement

Beta Kappa Gamma prides itself in its unique effort to promote growth as well as integration within society. The primary goal and essential mission is to strengthen bonds between existing peoples while becoming more active in the community.

Information

Beta Kappa Gamma is a national Multicultural fraternity, our brotherhood transcends all colors and creed. Established at the University of Texas at Austin (UT) on May 6, 1999 Beta Kappa Gamma's objectives include providing service to the community through various philanthropic activities, increasing awareness for world hunger relief, promoting academic scholarship, and creating a strong bond of brotherhood within its ranks. Beta Kappa Gamma was founded by twelve young men who came together with a single purpose - a vision of creating a timeless institution to cultivate brotherhood, knowledge, leadership.

Philanthropy

In its short presence at Baylor University, Beta Kappa Gamma has taken on two major philanthropies that are very close and dear to the Baylor community. With the support from organizations such as Multicultural Affairs, Multicultural Greek Council, the Student Government, Beta Kappa Gamma has been able to support its philanthropies to the best of its ability. We hope to continue to receive support from these organizations as we look forward to increase our contribution to the Baylor community.

Brothers Kollecting Gifts

Helping in the Christmas Season.

Each fall semester Beta Kappa Gamma focuses its efforts on promoting and taking steps towards diminishing poverty in Waco and around the world. This is a weeklong event that takes place towards the end of November when the Christmas spirit is at it’s highest. This week starts off with the brothers along with the Baylor community going out to the Boys and Girls Club of Waco and hanging out with the children. The second and third day focuses on collecting books and presents for these children. Most of these children do not receive presents from home due to the tight financial conditions. During these two days, a special event is held on Baylor’s campus where organizations from all around come together, donate and wrap presents. On the fourth day of the event the brothers go back to the Boys and Girls Club and give out these presents to these children. Not only that, every year a special surprise is also included where the brothers get together and do something nice for the Boys and Girls Club itself. Over the last few years the brothers have painted the gym floor, put up new basketball rims, provided the children with a new pool table and an extensive library. Over the next few years Beta Kappa Gamma hopes to continue to work with these children and provide them with the best educational resources to help them succeed.

Relay for Life

Curing cancer, one night at a time.

Ever since its establishment in 2010, every spring Beta Kappa Gamma has teamed up with the American Cancer Society and Relay for Life. For the last three years BKG has been the biggest donor for Relay for Life at Baylor University, donating over $2500, $4000 and $3000 respectively. This is large able in part due a charity dodge ball tournament BKG hosts right before Relay For Life called Dodge For a Cause. In the past this tournament has been very successful raising over $600 every single year. Each year Beta Kappa Gamma looks forward to cutting down the costs of the tournament and increasing its size to get more donations for American Cancer Society. Since its start in 2010, BKG has doubled the numbers of teams that sign up every year along with the donations as well.
